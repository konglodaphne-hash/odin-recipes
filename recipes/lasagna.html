Description

Making a classic lasagna involves layering a savory meat sauce, a creamy cheese filling, and tender pasta sheets before baking everything to bubbly, golden perfection.

I have gathered some top-rated recipes from the web, or you can follow the traditional Italian-style recipe provided below.

Ingredients

For the meat sauce

  • olive oil
  • ground beef
  • italian sausage
  • diced yellow onions
  • crushed tomatoes
  • garlic cloves
  • tomato paste

For cheese layering and mixture

  • egg
  • fresh parsley
  • ricotta cheese
  • mozzarella cheese
  • lasagna noodles

Steps

  1. Cook the meat sauce.

    Heat the olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef and Italian sausage, breaking the meat apart with a spoon until fully browned. Toss in the diced onion and garlic, cooking for 3 to 4 minutes until softened. Stir in the tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, Italian seasoning, sugar, salt, and pepper. Lower the heat and let the sauce simmer uncovered for 20 to 25 minutes to thicken.

  2. Prepare noodle and cheese filling

    While the sauce simmers, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the lasagna noodles for 8 to 10 minutes until al dente. Drain and rinse them under cold water to stop the cooking and prevent sticking. In a medium bowl, vigorously whisk together the ricotta cheese, egg, chopped parsley, grated Parmesan, and a pinch of salt and pepper until smooth and creamy. Pre-heat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  3. Assemble the layers

    Spread roughly 1/2 cup of the meat sauce across the bottom of your baking dish to keep the noodles from sticking. Lay 3 or 4 noodles flat over the sauce, overlapping them slightly.

  4. Bake and rest

    Lightly spray a sheet of aluminum foil with nonstick cooking spray and tent it over the dish, spray-side down, to keep the cheese from sticking. Bake covered at 375°F for 30 minutes.
